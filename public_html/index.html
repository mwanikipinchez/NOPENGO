About Us

National Organization of Peer Educators

National Organization of Peer Educators is an NGO in Kenya established in 2000 and received international mandate in 2007. NOPE works to improve the well-being of youth, people in the workplace, and the country's most at-risk populations by providing highly customized sexual reproductive health and social services using innovative peer centered and Social Behaviour Change Communication(SBCC) interventions that foster innovation.

For over 20 years, NOPE has built its portfolio in capacity building to both workplace and civil society organizations in more than 5 provinces of Kenya. It has maintained a good record and partner relations with remarkable program implementation record spanning a whole decade.

Partnership in Budget Advocacy for Enhanced and Sustained Epidemic Preparedness between GHAI and NOPE Kenya.

Global Health Advocacy Incubator (GHAI) and the National Organization of Peer Educators (NOPE) Kenya have collaborative efforts in advancing a Budget Advocacy framework with a strategic partnership focusing on enhancing and sustaining epidemic preparedness in Kenya. This nexus is based on the mission, goals, and previous successes in the field of public health and epidemic preparedness as well as mutual interest in addressing gaps in the current budgetary allocations for epidemic preparedness in Kenya with an emphasis on the need for sustainable funding and long-term strategies to combat emerging health threats.

These efforts are focused on practical implementation strategies, potential challenges, and opportunities for collaboration with existing health initiatives in Kenya as both organizations will explore strategies for resource mobilization, acknowledging the importance of securing funding and support for successful execution. GHAI and NOPE Kenya association is a pivotal strategic partnership aimed at improving epidemic preparedness through effective budget advocacy. Learn more->

U.S. Ambassador Engages with Kendubay Ward Safe Space, Commends USAID Nuru ya Mtoto Project Impact

In a powerful display of diplomatic collaboration, the U.S. Ambassador to Kenya visit to Kendubay Ward Safe Space, left an indelible mark on the community. The visit served as a testament to the unwavering commitment of the United States to transformative projects aimed at uplifting communities, particularly through initiatives like the USAID Nuru ya Mtoto project. The Ambassador engaged with the community to gain firsthand insights into the impact of the Nuru ya Mtoto project. The Safe Space provided a dynamic setting for a robust dialogue on the project's outcomes and its role in fostering positive change at both the community and county levels. Learn more->
